Prof. Wangari Maathai

A beacon of perseverance, peace and inspiration.

Here is a timeline of Prof. Wangari's life:

  • 1940: Born in Ihithe village, Nyeri district, Kenya.
  • 1956: Attended Loreto High School in Limuru.
  • 1960: As a beneficiary of the Kennedy Airlift, she studied in the United States, earning a bachelor's degree where she majored in Biology from Mount St. Scholastica in 1964.
  • 1966: Earned her Masters degree in biology from the University of Pittsburgh.
  • 1971: She became the first Eastern African woman to receive a PhD, her doctorate in veterinary anatomy from the University College of Nairobi, now the University of Nairobi.
  • 1977: Founded the Green Belt Movement which was instrumental in environmental protection in kenya.
  • 1989: Leads a series of protests and activism against a project to construct a building complex in Uhuru Park, a public recreational park in Nairobi, leading to its cancellation.
  • 1998: Leads a campaign against privatization of large areas of private land in Karura forest. Allocation of all private land was banned by the then president Daniel Arap Moi.
  • 2002: Wins the parliamentary seat for Tetu constituency.
  • 2004: Becomes the first African woman to win the nobel peace prize.
  • 2011: Dies due to complications arising from ovarian cancer.

She went on to win many awards throughout her life and even posthumously. She is buried at the Wangari Maathai Institute for Peace and Environmental Studies in Nairobi.

"In the course of history, there comes a time when humanity is called to shift to a new level of consciousness, to reach a higher moral ground. A time when we have to shed our fear and give hope to each other. That time is now."

--Prof. Wangari Maathai.
